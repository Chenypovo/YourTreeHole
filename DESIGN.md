## Overview

Treehole is a warm, intimate space — like a friend's living room, not a cold tech product. The visual identity draws from forest greens and warm earth tones, evoking growth, trust, and the feeling of being heard.

## Colors

The palette centers on forest greens for trust and calm, with warm amber and terracotta accents for warmth and personality.

- **Primary (#2D6A4F):** Deep forest green — the core identity. Used for headers, primary buttons, and user chat bubbles.
- **Primary Light (#40916C):** A softer green for hover states and subtle highlights.
- **Secondary (#52B788):** Fresh sage green for progress bars, badges, and secondary elements.
- **Tertiary (#D4A373):** Warm amber — the "sunlight through trees" accent. Used sparingly for special moments.
- **Neutral (#FEFAE0):** Warm cream background, softer than pure white. The canvas for conversations.
- **Dark (#1B4332):** Deep pine for text and emphasis.
- **Accent (#E76F51):** Terracotta for delete buttons and warnings — warm but clear.

## Typography

Clean and readable. Serif for the title (warmth, personality), sans-serif for everything else (clarity, modern). Monospace for labels and technical details.

## Layout

Generous whitespace. Chat bubbles float with comfortable padding. Sidebar is warm-toned but not distracting. Content breathes.

## Do's and Don'ts

- **Do** use warm, natural colors that feel inviting
- **Do** keep generous spacing — this is a cozy space, not a dashboard
- **Do** use rounded corners to soften the interface
- **Don't** use harsh blues or corporate grays
- **Don't** crowd the interface with too many elements
- **Don't** use pure white backgrounds — prefer warm cream (#FEFAE0)
